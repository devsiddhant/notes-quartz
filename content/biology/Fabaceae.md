---
title: Fabaceae
---

### i. Distribution
Cosmopolitan in distribution, abundant in tropical and subtropical regions of the world.
### ii. Habit and Habitat
Usually annual, herbs, climbers, twinners, lianas, perennial shrubs and trees; mostly mesophytes, rarely xerophytes (Acacia) and hydrophytes; wild or cultivated

## Vegetative characters
### i. Root
Taproot and branched; roots contain nodules that bear nitrogen fixing bacteria
### ii.Stem
Erect or climbing, herbaceous or woody, twining, climbing stem with leaf tendrils, branched, solid or fistular, cylindrical, glabrous or pubescent
### iii.Leaf
Cauline and Ramal, alternate, whorled or opposite, usually compound or simple, petiolate or sessile, stipulate, mostly foliaceous (*Pisum Sativum*), pulvinus leaf base, unicostate reticulate venation, sometimes leaflets are modified into tendrils
## Floral characters
### i.Inflorescence
Racemose type, usually raceme, solitary, axillary or terminal

### ii.Flower
Bracteate, pedicillate, bisexual, complete, zygomorphic, pentamerous, hypogynous
### iii. Calyx
Sepals 5, gamosepalous, usually campanulate, aestivation valvate or ascending imbricate, usually persistent, sepaloid, inferior
