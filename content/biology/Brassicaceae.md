### i. Distribution
Cosmopolitan in distribution, mainly disributed in tropical and temperate regions of Northern Hemisphere.
### ii. Habit and Habitat
Usually herbs, rarely undershrub (Farsetia) and shrubs; annual or bienneial or perennial ; mostly mesophytes, rarely hydrophytes (Nasturtium officinale); wild or cultivated

## Vegetative characters
### i. Root
Taproot and branched; sometimes modified into fusiform root (Radish), Napiform root (Turnip) etc.
### ii.Stem
Erect, branched, cylindrical, glabrous or pubescent, herbaceous, solid
### iii.Leaf
Radical, Cauline and Ramal, simple or rarely compound (sim saag), exstipulate, petiolate, sub-sessile, sessile; margin entire or wavy or serrate; glabrous or pubescent, unicostate reticulate venation
## Floral characters
### i.Inflorescence
Racemose inflorescence, typically raceme

### ii.Flower
Ebracteate, pedicillate, bisexual, complete, Actinomorphic, sometimes zygomorphic (*Iberis amara*), tetramerous, hypogynous, perigynous in *Lepidium sativum*
### iii. Calyx
Sepals four, polysepalous, arranged in two whorls of two each, imbricate aestivation, sepalloid or petalloid, inferior
### iv.Corolla
Petals four, polypetalous, cruciform, clawed, alternating with sepals  valvate aestivation, inferior
### v. Androecium
Stamens six, polyandrous, tetradynamous i.e. outer two stamens shorter and inner four stamens longer,anther dithecous, basifixed or dorsifixed, introrse, green nectaries present at the base, inferior
### vi. Gynoecium
Bicarpellary, syncarpous, unilocular but becomes bilocular due to the formation of false septum called replum, ovules many, ovary superior, placentation parietal
### vii.Fruit
Siliqua or silicula
### viii.Pollination:
Self or cross, entomophilous
### viii.Seed
Non-endospermic, cotyledons 2

## Diagnostic Characters
Herbaceous plants with sulphur smelling glucosinolates.
 1. Exstipulate leaves.
 2. Fruit siliqua or silicula
 3. Green nectaries are associated with stamen.
 4. Tetramerous, polysepalous, polypetalous characters.
 5. Cruciform flowers
 6. Bicarpellary, syncarpous ovary with parietal placentation

## Some plants of Brassicaceae family
1. *Brassica campestris* - Mustard
2. *Lepidium sativum* - Chansoor
3. *Brassica nigra* - Chaalne (Medicinal value)
4. *Raphanus sativus* - Radish (Food)
